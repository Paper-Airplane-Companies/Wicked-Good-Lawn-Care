Hello, everyone! I'm Brandon Labonte, the owner of Wicked Good Lawn Care. Today, I want to talk about a topic that's close to my heart: safety. Specifically, the importance of being cautious with lawn care machinery and using proper protective equipment (PPE). As someone who's been in the lawn care business for years, I've seen firsthand how essential it is to prioritize safety to prevent injuries. Let me share some insights and hypothetical situations to underscore why PPE is non-negotiable.

It's easy to become complacent when working with lawn care machinery, especially if you've been doing it for a long time. However, complacency can lead to severe injuries. Take, for example, the case of using a lawnmower. Many people assume that mowing the lawn is a simple, straightforward task. But imagine this: you're mowing your lawn on a sunny day, and a small stone is hidden in the grass. As you pass over it, the mower's blades propel the stone at high speed. Without proper PPE, this stone can cause serious injury.

## The Flying Debris

Imagine not wearing safety glasses during this mowing session. That flying stone hits your eye, causing immediate pain and potentially leading to permanent damage. However, wearing a simple pair of safety glasses could have prevented this injury. Safety glasses are designed to protect your eyes from flying debris, ensuring that even if the worst happens, you're shielded from harm.

## The Importance of Hearing Protection

Another critical aspect of lawn care safety is protecting your hearing. Lawn care machinery, like leaf blowers and chainsaws, can be extremely loud. Prolonged exposure to these high decibel levels can cause hearing loss over time. Let's consider another scenario: you're trimming a hedge with an electric trimmer. The noise from the trimmer is constant and loud. Without ear protection, this can gradually damage your hearing.

**Hypothetical Situation: Gradual Hearing Loss**

Imagine working for years without ear protection. One day, you realize that you can't hear as well as you used to. Conversations become harder to follow, and you miss out on the subtle sounds that were once clear. This gradual hearing loss could have been prevented with a simple pair of earplugs or earmuffs. These PPE items are designed to reduce the noise levels reaching your ears, allowing you to work safely without risking your hearing.

## Protecting Your Hands and Feet

When working with sharp and heavy machinery, protecting your hands and feet is crucial. Lawn care equipment like hedge trimmers, chainsaws, and even lawnmowers can cause severe injuries if not handled properly. Consider a scenario where you're using a chainsaw without wearing gloves. Chainsaws can kick back unexpectedly, and without gloves, your hands are vulnerable to serious cuts and abrasions.

**Foot Protection**

Similarly, wearing proper footwear is essential. Steel-toed or composite toed boots are a great when operating heavy machinery. Picture yourself moving a lawnmower, and it accidentally rolls over your foot. Regular sneakers won't provide much protection, but steel-toed boots can prevent serious injuries by shielding your toes from the mower's weight. They also shield your feet from line trimmer line and debris.

Investing in PPE might seem like an extra expense, but it's a small price to pay for your safety and well-being. Safety glasses, ear protection, gloves, and boots are readily available and designed to keep you safe while you work. At Wicked Good Lawn Care, we make it a point to use PPE not just because it's a good practice, but because it's essential for preventing injuries.

Creating a safety culture starts with education and practice. I encourage everyone on my team to always wear the appropriate PPE and to never take shortcuts when it comes to safety. We regularly hold safety meetings to discuss potential hazards and how to mitigate them. By fostering a culture that prioritizes safety, we ensure that everyone goes home uninjured at the end of the day.

In the world of lawn care, safety should never be an afterthought. The right PPE can mean the difference between a routine day and a trip to the emergency room. Whether you're a professional landscaper or a homeowner tending to your yard, always take the time to use the proper protective equipment. It's a small step that makes a big difference.

Stay safe out there, and remember: a well-maintained lawn is beautiful, but nothing is more important than your health and safety. Excellence Starts at the Root!
